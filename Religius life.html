Minor seminaries exist to develop the seeds of vocations. Students are prepared through special religious formation, particularly through appropriate spiritual direction, to follow Christ the Redeemer with generosity of spirit and purity of heart. This intentional focus on spiritual growth helps young men discern their calling and explore their faith and spirituality.

Daily Spiritual Practices: Within the seminary environment, students engage in daily Mass, communal prayer, and the availability of the sacraments. These practices foster a sound spiritual life, including: Recognizing the human need for redemption and the transformative power of Christ’s sacrifice, learning various ways of prayer, establishing a rule of life, and engaging in spiritual reading, cultivating personal conversion and love for others, aligning personal beliefs with public behavior and embracing the teachings of the Church, reflecting on their call to priesthood, embracing a simple lifestyle, understanding the importance of obedience and participating in the Church’s prayer life and nurturing a sense of justice and compassion.

Saint John the apostle is our patron saint. Saint John the Apostle's mission was pivotal in shaping the Catholic Church. As a close companion of Jesus, he authored the Gospel of John, emphasizing Christ's divinity and salvation. His three epistles provided pastoral guidance and addressed theological concerns. John's leadership in Ephesus strengthened early Christian communities amidst adversity. His emphasis on love as central to Christian life inspires seminarians at NKUMBA. John's mystical experiences, like the Book of Revelation, offer insights into divine truths. His legacy includes evangelization, pastoral care, and theological reflection. John's profound intimacy with Jesus enriched his teachings and spiritual insights. His enduring impact continues to shape Catholic faith and practice in NKUMBA MINOR SEMINARY. Saint John's contributions remain integral to the Church's theology, spirituality, and mission. Saint John the Apostle is celebrated in the Catholic Church on December 27th. This day commemorates his life, teachings, and contributions to the Christian faith but is celebrated on different dates in NKUMBA MINOR SEMINARY because on December 27 seminarians are in holidays.
